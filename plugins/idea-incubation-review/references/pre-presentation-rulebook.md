# 999 Pre-Presentation Rulebook — distilled

Source: "The 999 Pre-Presentation Rulebook" (999 Internal Incubator · Pre-Proposal Gate · v1.0). Self-check every venture runs **before** presenting to Dmitry's weekly review. Sits on top of the PIH 1st Pass (page 09) — sharpens an idea, does not approve it (only Dmitry approves).

Distilled criteria + thresholds. The bar: real adoption, an honest market, a plan you'd stake resources on.

**Verdict states:** `Ready` (meets rule, evidence in hand) · `Refine` (close, fix gap, re-check) · `Rethink` (fails a hard rule, reshape or park).

---

## Hard gate (outranks everything)

**Software-first only.** Delivered and operated as software through profitability — no headcount, no manual process, no physical presence. Fail here = **Rethink** no matter how strong the rest.

## Four milestone requirements

Plan must credibly show each. Rough numbers fine, must exist and add up.

| When | Requirement |
|---|---|
| 3-year path | $2M–$20M ARR, one named revenue driver |
| Day 30 | viable MVP live — one small team, approved platform |
| Day 120 | 20 SMB or 1,000 consumer users + first paying customers |
| Day 180 | monthly cash flow ≥ 0 incl. labor |

## Validation ladder

Earn each rung before the next. Real adoption at one level = ticket to test above.

1. Personal tool — you use it yourself, by choice, repeatedly.
2. Team tool — squad adopts without being told.
3. Department tool — whole dept opens it daily.
4. Other departments — usefulness travels.
5. External users — the commercial test: outsiders want it and pay.

Full internal adoption required before commercial endorsement. Internal love ≠ outside customer paying. Promotion re-proves all 5 commercial criteria from scratch.

## 20-item self-assessment (the scored checklist)

Tick only what is genuinely true **with evidence**. Grouped A–E. Verdict by % checked.

**A. Foundations (3)**
1. Software-first confirmed — no headcount/manual process to operate through profitability.
2. All four milestone requirements have a credible plan.
3. An accountable Idea Owner is named.

**B. Adoption evidence (5)**
4. Real usage data — people choose to use it (repeat usage, not sign-ups/surveys).
5. Adoption happened without being mandated.
6. People use it intuitively, little/no training.
7. Success metrics defined before onboarding.
8. Right ladder rung has full adoption before climbing.

**C. Market & competition (4)**
9. Target market and industry clearly defined ("everyone" is not a market).
10. Competitive analysis done — rivals, pricing, gaps.
11. Insight gathered from relevant internal departments.
12. Market-local perspective considered where idea targets other regions.

**D. Business & growth (4)**
13. Business plan and revenue model ready.
14. Commercial viability backed by organic demand (unprompted interest, inbound, waitlist pull).
15. Cost of sale low — no per-customer customization.
16. A hook or point of virality identified.

**E. Scope & polish (4)**
17. No enterprise functionality built ahead of commercial approval.
18. A plan exists for customer support and product-market fit (the two killers).
19. Product name correctly spelled and specific to the use.
20. A runnable demo ready to lead with.

## Bands (% of 20 checked-with-evidence)

- **Ready** — ≥ 90%.
- **Refine** — ≥ 60% and < 90%.
- **Rethink** — < 60%.

**Software-first fail overrides to Rethink regardless of %.** Every item weighs equally; only non-linear rule is the hard gate.

## Red flags / hard fails

- Adoption only under mandate → not validation.
- No business plan / revenue model → not ready to present.
- No traction / not pulling users → drop it.
- Enterprise functionality before commercial approval → scope violation.

## Presenter deliverables (must bring)

Software-first confirmation · named accountable Idea Owner · milestone plan (30/120/180 + 3-yr ARR w/ named driver) · real usage/adoption data · ladder position · target market + industry · competitive analysis · internal-department insight · business plan + revenue model · evidence of organic demand · low cost-of-sale design · named virality/growth hook · customer-support + PMF plan · correctly-spelled use-specific product name · runnable demo to lead with.
