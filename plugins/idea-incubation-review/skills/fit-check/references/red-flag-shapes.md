# Red-flag shapes — does the idea ride the 999 clock, or fight it?

The caps ($10k labor / $5k marketing), the clock (30/120/180-day), and the software-first hard gate act as a filter. Most classic startup red flags are business *shapes* that structurally fight those constraints. Name the idea's shape, sum the friction, pull the matching do's & don'ts.

**Friction:** negative = rides the clock, positive = fights it.
**Shape read (motion + market tags only):** sum ≤ 0 → **rides** · 1–3 → **mixed** · ≥ 4 → **fights**.

A "fights" read is not a rejection — it means reshape the wedge (narrow the segment, remove the labor, find standalone value) before Dmitry. Map it to a REFINE or RETHINK note, never a block.

## Motion tags

### B2B — SMB · friction −1
- DO target a narrow vertical/role where the pain is budgeted and recurring — fastest path to the 20-account gate.
- DO get to a **paid** pilot, not a free one. 20 paying SMB accounts beats 200 free trials for the 120-day gate.
- DO price higher than feels comfortable — SMB B2B tolerates real prices; it's the quickest line to $20k/mo.
- DON'T sell to a champion who must escalate every purchase — that's an enterprise cycle in disguise.
- DON'T count LOIs, pilots, or "very interested" as revenue.
- WATCH: procurement/security reviews eating the 30-day clock ⇒ picked too far up-market.

### B2B — Enterprise · friction +3
- WATCH: sales cycles routinely exceed 180 days — fights the whole clock.
- DON'T let security/legal/procurement/integration break the 30-day MVP and the low-labor caps.
- DON'T mistake one signed logo for repeatable acquisition.
- DO de-risk down-market first; move up-market after spin-off.
- DO escalate early — enterprise data/integration needs are explicit 999 triggers.

### B2C — Consumer · friction 0
- DO pick a segment with genuine willingness to pay — 1,000 users is a lot to reach organically and they must pay by day 120.
- DO instrument retention from day one — consumer fit is repeat use (the Sean Ellis signal).
- DO find an organic/owned channel that fits inside the $5k cap.
- DON'T chase vanity metrics (downloads, signups).
- DON'T build a business that only works with paid acquisition — CAC out-runs the cap and the profit threshold.
- DON'T run "grow now, monetize later" — fails the 120-day paying-revenue requirement.
- WATCH: novelty churns before day 180.

### B2B2C · friction +1
- WATCH: two customers — the business that pays and the end-user who must love it. Both, or neither works.
- WATCH: chicken-and-egg between partner and users; sequencing inside 120 days is brutal.
- DON'T depend on one partner as a single point of failure with slow, integration-heavy onboarding.
- DO run the Sean Ellis test on **end-users**; validate willingness-to-pay with the business buyer.
- DO land a partner who already has the users, so you inherit distribution.

### Marketplace / two-sided · friction +2
- WATCH: liquidity cold-start — hardest shape to validate in 120 days.
- DON'T subsidize both sides to fake demand — fights the $5k cap.
- WATCH: thin take-rate revenue early; $20k/mo is slow.
- DO constrain to one geography/category/segment to manufacture liquidity in a small pond.
- DO ship "single-player mode" first — value to one side before the other exists.

### Community-reliant · friction +2
- WATCH: empty-room problem — no value until critical mass.
- DON'T rely on human moderation/curation — ongoing labor threatens the software-first hard gate.
- DON'T assume engagement equals revenue; the 120-day gate needs cash.
- WATCH: incumbents (Discord, Reddit, FB groups) have winner-take-all gravity.
- DO anchor on a tool useful to a single user first; let community emerge as a layer.
- DO automate moderation as software, or the labor breaks the model.

### PLG / self-serve · friction −2
- DO lean in — the motion the caps and clock were built for: fast time-to-value, low labor, measurable retention.
- DO instrument activation (first hit of core value) and cut time-to-that.
- DO use the Sean Ellis loop to find the activated "very disappointed" cohort, then widen the funnel to that profile.
- DON'T add a sales team to rescue a product that won't self-activate — fix activation.

### Sales-led / high touch · friction +2
- DON'T build a model where profit requires ongoing human selling — collides with the hard gate and the labor cap.
- DON'T mistake founder hustle for a repeatable channel.
- WATCH: 180-day cash-flow-positive *including labor* is very hard when the labor is a sales team.
- DO treat heavy early sales as temporary scaffolding to learn the pitch.
- WATCH: if it genuinely can't sell without high touch, it may belong on the Internal Track — or it's a RETHINK.
- **Stage matters — distinguish two very different things:** sales effort as the *path to profitability* (red flag, fights the software-first gate) vs. sales & marketing to *scale a product that already has early-adopter PMF and is cash-flow-positive* (a legitimate later-stage growth lever, per the roadmap in `sean-ellis-fit.md`). Penalize the former; the latter is fine and expected — just not a 1st-Pass concern.

## Market tags

### Red ocean (crowded) · friction +1
- DON'T enter with no wedge — no wedge means a price war and a CAC you can't afford.
- DON'T pitch "like X but cheaper/better"; feature parity is a trap.
- DO win one underserved segment completely before widening.
- DO compete on a 10× axis (speed, workflow, pricing model), not a longer feature list.
- DO let the Sean Ellis "very disappointed" cohort tell you which niche loves you enough to switch.

### Blue ocean / new category · friction 0
- WATCH: no existing budget line ⇒ long education/adoption, fights the 120-day clock.
- WATCH: "no competitors" sometimes means "no market" — validate the problem first.
- DO anchor to a pain people already pay to solve badly, so you inherit a budget.
- DO keep the wedge razor-narrow so time-to-value stays inside 30 days.

### Niche / underserved · friction −2
- DO exploit — narrow high-pain segments are the fastest route to the 20-SMB gate and a clean Sean Ellis 40%.
- DO own the niche completely, then expand adjacencies post-spin-off.
- WATCH: confirm the niche still models to $2–20M ARR, or it fails the revenue-path gate.

### Network-effects dependent · friction +2
- WATCH: same cold-start risk as marketplaces/communities.
- DON'T assume virality; design standalone value that works before the network exists.
- DO give a single user a reason to return at N=1.

### Regulated / sensitive data · friction +2
- DON'T touch sensitive/regulated data or integrate a core system without escalating to Dmitry first — both are explicit triggers.
- WATCH: compliance reviews break the 30-day MVP and the low-labor model.
- DO validate with synthetic/limited-scope data first.
- DO consider the Internal Track proving ground before the commercial clock if regulation is central.

## Always-on rules (regardless of shape)

- Charge from day one — the 120-day gate needs paying customers, not signups.
- Write a falsifiable hypothesis before building.
- Validate the problem before the solution (don't mention the solution during problem validation).
- Ship the smallest useful MVP in 30 days.
- Never confuse shipping with fit; a launched product isn't a success until milestone evidence is in.
- Never let one big customer turn the MVP into consulting-ware.
- Never treat "I'd probably use it" as demand.
- Never defer the kill/KIV decision because work started — sunk cost is not evidence.
