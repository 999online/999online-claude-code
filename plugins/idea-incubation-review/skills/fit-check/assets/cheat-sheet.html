40%"very disappointed" ≈ you likely have fit. Below it, you're refining — not scaling.
  • Only survey users who reached core value (used it ≥2×). Tourists add noise.
  • Segment the "very disappointed" — that's your High-Expectation Customer. Build for them.
  • Ask fanatics "main benefit?"; ask near-misses "what one change makes it a must-have?"

Rules that hold regardless

The load-bearing 999 discipline

Always do

  • Charge from day one. The 120-day gate needs paying customers, not signups.
  • Write a falsifiable hypothesis before you build. Evidence over opinion.
  • Validate the problem before the solution. Don't mention your solution while testing the problem.
  • Ship the smallest useful MVP in 30 days. Defer polish, permissions, deep reporting.
  • Keep a "what would make me kill this?" answer ready at every review.

Never do

  • Confuse shipping with fit. A launched product isn't a success until the evidence is in.
  • Let one big customer turn the MVP into consulting-ware.
  • Treat "I'd probably use it" as demand. Look for payment, waitlist, repeat use.
  • Defer the kill/KIV decision because work started. Sunk cost is not evidence.
  • Need labor to reach profit. That fails the software-first hard gate — Rethink now.
